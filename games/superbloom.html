Superbloom

Tile Strategy Game  ·  with Josh Hirshfield, Phil Robiverl, Diganta Ghorai  ·  2022–2023

A competitive tile-placement game about butterflies and wildflowers. Inspired by California's natural superblooms — the sudden seasonal wildflower clusters that pull butterflies in from all over — players take the role of butterflies picking which plants will flourish most.

Concept

Every few years, a wet California winter produces a superbloom — a brief, overwhelming carpet of wildflowers that draws butterflies in enormous numbers. Superbloom (the game) condenses that into three rounds of quiet, strategic betting on which flowers will thrive.

Gameplay

  • Players predict which plants will flourish most to accumulate points.
  • If multiple players pick the same plant, points are split — shared picks create real tension.
  • Flower tiles can be revealed to activate unique abilities, or kept hidden as face-down seeds for end-of-game bonuses.
  • Three rounds, each ending in a "super bloom event" that flips all tiles face-up, creating a striking floral tableau at the close.

My Role

Illustrated the flower cards and contributed to visual design + prototyping. The art drove a lot of the final feel — cards had to read quickly in play but hold up to being laid out as a full blooming field at the end of each round.
