Patti Veedu

4th-Year Design Project  ·  NID Ahmedabad  ·  2021

A 2D point-and-click adventure prototype. Two sisters visit their grandmother and find her missing from the house — their search to locate her leads to unexpected discoveries about who she really is.

Concept

Patti Veedu started from a small observation — that our parents and grandparents are more textured than the role they play in our lives. The game uses puzzle-solving as a way of uncovering character: as you search the house for your grandmother, the objects you find, the drawers you open, and the photos you piece together tell you who she is.

My Role

Designed and built the game end-to-end as my 4th-year Design Project at the National Institute of Design, Ahmedabad. Illustrated all backgrounds and characters, wrote the narrative, and implemented the point-and-click mechanics from scratch.

  • Background illustration — four hand-drawn rooms.
  • Character design — grandmother and the two sisters.
  • Writing, puzzle design, and implementation.
