Visual Novel  ·  with Nyusha Impolski, Jet Vellinga, Chros Wang

A visual novel about intrusive thoughts and anxiety — and about a protagonist who can't confess to the person they love. As everyday life unfolds, repetitive anxious thoughts push in from the margins; the player has to decide what to do with them.

About

Listen follows a protagonist who can't quite confess romantic feelings to Varsha. The game plays out in small everyday scenes — walking, waiting, texting — with anxious thoughts surfacing in red text throughout. Player choices appear as thought bubbles rather than dialogue options, keeping the decision-making internal.

Midway through the story, Varsha stops responding. The texting mechanic becomes the primary lens: messages left on read, draft bubbles typed and deleted, the quiet panic of not knowing what happened. Her disappearance is revealed through the messaging layer itself.

Gameplay

  • Intrusive thoughts surface in red text layered over the main scene.
  • Player choices appear as thought bubbles — internal, not spoken.
  • Texting mechanic integrated into the scene flow, not as a separate menu.
  • Narrative reveal delivered through the messaging interface itself.

My Role

Co-designed with the team and led narrative and UI implementation. Integrated the red-text intrusive-thought system with scene pacing so that anxious loops feel like they're interrupting, not scripted.
